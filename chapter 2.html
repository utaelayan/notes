Indian Economy During 1950-1990

Economic system

It refers to an arrangement by which central problem of economy are solved

    The three central problems are:
  1. What to produce?
  2. How to produce?
  3. For whom to produce?

Types of economic systems

  1. Capitalist/Market economy
    • Profit earning
    • Controlled and operated by private producers
  2. Socialist/Centrally-planned economy
    • Welfare
    • Controlled by government
  3. Mixed economy

Economic Planning

Economic Planning can be defined as making major economic decisions determined by authority on the basis of national priorities.

The Planning Commision of India was set up in 1950 under the chairman or prime minister. Jawaharlal Nehru is the first chairperson of planning commission

note

On 1st January 2015, the planning commission was replaced by NITi Aayog

5 Year Plans

For achieving the objectives of Planning commission in which a detailed scheme, program and strategy is mentioned in a document known as a 5 year plan.

5 year plans started in the year of 1950-51 to 1955-56

Goal of 5 year plans

  1. Growth
    • It refers to increase in the country's capacity to produce goods and services.
    • Larger stock of productive capital
    • Large infrastructure
    • Increase in the efficienct of capital
  2. Modernization
    • Adoption of new technology
    • Increase in the production of goods and services
    • It also includes changes in social outlook. Such as equal rights to women etc.
  3. Self reliance
    • It means avoiding importing those goods which could be produced within the country itself.
    • It reduces our dependence on foregin countries
    • Dependence on imported food , technology and capital may affect our sovereignty
  4. Equity
    • A country can have high growth rate, modern technology but also have a high number of poor.
    • Benefits of economic prosperity should reach the poor sections.
    • Every Indian should be able to meet basic needs.
    • Inequality in the distribution of wealth should be reduced.
