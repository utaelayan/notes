Indian Economy at the Eve of Independece

Introduction

The Indian Economic Development is an important part of our country, the basic features of indian economy and its process and progress of development.

The origin of British rule

the british rule over india changed the economic system of india. the formation of british empire in india was in 1757 and around 200 years british rule badly destroyed the indian economic system. but finally after 200 years india got independence on 15th august 1947

Condition of IED under British rule

  1. backward economy
  2. slow growth of agriculture development
  3. de-inustrialisation
  4. low level of prouctivity
  5. backward education system

Agricultural Sector during Independece

the large portion of population engages in agricultural sector. nearly about 85% of country population lives in village area and they are directly or indirectly from agricultural sector

following are the reasons for stagnation in the agricultural sector

  • Zamindari system(first established in Bengal presidency)
  • Irrigation facilities
  • Commercialisation of agriculture
  • Lack of investment
  • Small and fragmented lands

Industrial sector during independece

  1. de-inustrialisation and decline of handicraft industries:

    2. british government systematically destroyed handicraft industries and no modern industries were allowed to come up because the primary motive of the british government was:

    • to get the raw material from india at cheap rate to be used by upcoming new industrial in britiain
    • to sell finished products of british industries in indian market for higher prices
  2. adverse effect of decline of handicraft industries:
    • high level of unemployment
    • import of finished goods
  3. lack of capital goods industries
  4. limited role of public sector

Foreign sector during independece

the policies adopted by colonial government adversely affected the structure , composition and volume of india's foreign trade

    The British Government adopted the following policies:
  1. exporter of primary product and importer of finished goods
  2. monopoly control of british rule
  3. drain of indian wealth during colonian rule

Decay of Handicraft Industries

  • Discriminatory tariff policy
  • Competition from machine made products
  • Dissapearance of princely courts
  • Introduction of railways

Slow growth of modern Industries

  • Iron and Steel industries set up (TISCO-1907)
  • Lack of capital goods industries
  • Limited participation of government
  • Less contribution in GDP

India's Foreign Trade

  • Britain maintained monopoly control over India's exports and imports.
  • The opening of Suez Canal(1869) further intensified British control over India's foreign trade
  • More than 50% of India's foreign trade was restricted to Britain while the remaining was allowed with a few other contries like China, Persia etc.

The positive contributions made by the Britishers in India were:

  • The development of roads and railways(1850) opened up new opportunities for economic and social growth.
  • British rule helped the Indian economy to shift from a barter system of exchange to a monetary system of exchange.
  • Developed modern system of communication
  • Modernization of agriculture: The British introduced modern agricultural practices, such as the cultivation of cash crops like tea, coffee, and cotton, which helped in the development of the agriculture sector in India.
